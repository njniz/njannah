Gophish Project

Phishing attacks trick people into sharing sensitive information or clicking harmful links, posing a big risk to organizations. For my Industrial Training project, I’m using Gophish Website to create fake phishing emails to teach employees how to recognize and avoid these scams. The goal is to raise awareness, help employees understand the tricks cybercriminals use, and improve overall cybersecurity in the organization.


The Checking KPIs phishing email project was designed to test and increase awareness among users regarding phishing email threats. The project’s specific purpose was to obtain sensitive information, such as staff IDs and passwords, through a simulated phishing attack.


In this project nine groups have been targeted with phishing emails and want to see if the staff were aware with phishing or caught by this phishing.From this project,we found 97.3% of employees at LZNK probably ignore emails because employee doubt the authenticity of the email sent, which results in employees not opening it. In addition, 2.7% of users were affected user because employee opened the email. Of those 2.7% , 2.4% have clicked on a link in an email and 1.7% have sent personal information.


This conclusion shows that most employees tend to ignore e-mails of doubtful authenticity, but a small number of employees are still exposed to risk by clicking on links included in e-mails.
